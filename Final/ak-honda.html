Case Study: Honda App

An app designed to help record your outdoor progress

For this project, we were to research an area of interest that needed an app or website to help with an issue that is present. This issue was communication in a car dealership between the Sales team and the Detail team. The solution was an app that would work for communication and organization.

Role: UX Designer

School: Brigham Young University - Idaho

Date Completed: April 2021

The Problem and Idea:

I grew up around car dealerships a lot because that is where my dad worked. I would often hear about things that could be better around the shop, more recently, I heard of the complications of communication between the Sales team and the Detail team. With the help of my dad and brother, we came up with this app idea to fix the problem that they saw. The app will be designed for Car dealerships, to allow better communication between the Sales team and the Detail team. This will allow the two to communicate their ideas, problems, and be able to plan out and keep in touch to keep the day going smoothly.

Research:

From the research I conducted, there was not an app that covers the areas that I want to in this app that I want to develop. There are already messaging app such as Facebook Messenger, Whatsapp, and Google numbers. However, I plan to create this app so it can be personalized to each dealership.

Audience:

The main audience would be the employees of the car dealership. This app would allow the Salesmen and the Detail Team, or the shop workers to be able to work faster and more effectively so that they can better meet the needs of their customers.

What I Learned:

During the process of developing this app, I was able to better understand how to design an app to function in and for a work type environment. I had to do an ample amount of research, both looking at other already created apps that function similarly to how I wanted my app to function, and also talk people in the industry that I was working to design an app for. This helped a great deal because there were things that I looked passed. I learned more about design and how to create things so they would work well and be user friendly.

What I Would Change:

There were some parts of the design of the app that I wasn’t very satisfied with. While they worked in the technical sense, they could have been more visually appealing. Also, while conducting the usability testing with the owner of a car dealership, also my father, he was pleased with the overall layout of the app, but also explained the other needed aspects of the app if it were to be a real app. These weren’t things I could add in now, but they would be needed to help the dealership become more organized.

TheShop is an app inspired by the needs of the Car Dealership that my dad owns. Growing up my siblings and I would always spend a little time a week there and it became very familiar. Recently, my brother worked with my dad and had a few ideas to better organize the shop. This included better communication between the employees

The shop is designed to connect to the system that the dealership already uses, and helps with organization and communication even on your phone.

Key Features:

  • Add cars directly to your Shop Inventory
  • Easily send information to other employees efficiently
  • Move swiftly between pages to find cars, new, used, or sold

Molding Through Usability Testing:

TheShop went through three phases of Usability Testing, allowing me to see where I can improve the design to make it more easily managed. These Usability Tests were designed to test key features of the app. It was reassuring to watch these tests as I saw where improvement was necessary and where the design was working.

These senarios were:

  • Adding a car to the dealership inventory
  • Finding a specific car in the dealership inventory/car lot
  • Sending the specific car in the app messaging system
  • Finding the messaging feature of the app
  • Sending a message
  • Marking a car as sold
